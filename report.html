How to Add It to Your Website

IMPORTANT: You may need help for this first-time setup (or you can try to follow the guide below).

Option 1: Free Google Translate Widget (Recommended)

This is completely free and works great. Here's what you (or Matthew) need to do:

Step 1

Open Your CoffeeCup HTML Editor

Open CoffeeCup and load your website's home page (called index.html )

Step 2

Find the Right Spot in Your Code

Look for a line near the top that says <body> or <body style=...

TIP: Press Ctrl+F on your keyboard and type "body" to find it quickly

Step 3

Copy This Code

Copy everything in this gray box:

<div id="google_translate_element"></div>
<script type="text/javascript">
function googleTranslateElementInit() {
  new google.translate.TranslateElement(
    {pageLanguage: 'en'},
    'google_translate_element'
  );
}
</script>
<script type="text/javascript"
  src="//translate.google.com/translate_a/element.js?cb=googleTranslateElementInit">
</script>
Step 4

Paste It In

Right after the <body> line, press Enter to make a new line, then paste the code you copied.

Step 5

Save and Upload

Save your file in CoffeeCup, then upload it to your website the same way you normally do.

Step 6

Test It!

Go to your website in a web browser (Chrome) and look for the language dropdown menu. Try selecting Spanish or another language!

Step 7

Add to All Your Other Pages

Repeat steps 1-5 for every page on your website. Yes, this is time-consuming, but it only needs to be done once!

⚠️ Things to Know:

  • Google Banner: A small "Powered by Google" banner will appear at the top. This is normal for the free version.
  • Not Perfect Translations: Machine translation isn't perfect, but it's good enough for people to understand your content.
  • Won't Help with Google Search: The free version won't make you show up in other languages on Google searches. That would require a paid service (see below).
